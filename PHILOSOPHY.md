### 🛡️ Why Tycho?

In an increasingly centralized web, our digital lives and data are often delegated to opaque proprietary silos and exposed to government surveillance. Self-hosting is the obvious answer to break free, but it often feels like a constant struggle against entropy: oscillating between fragile installation scripts and the over-engineering of industrial orchestrators (designed for the cloud) that are completely unsuited for the scale of a personal server.

Tycho was born from a philosophy of pragmatic adaptation to hardware reality. Rather than fighting chaos with suffocating virtual complexity, the tool proposes building a resilient, organic, and sovereign infrastructure.

Much like an engineering station assembling modules of autonomy in a hostile environment, Tycho builds your ecosystem service by service. Whether securing your historical archives or powering your streaming platforms, it orchestrates your applications by relying strictly on free open-source orchestration tools. By enforcing a *rootless-first* architecture, Tycho forges an impermeable and stoic shield around each container, guaranteeing uncompromising isolation and security.

More than just a simple command-line utility, Tycho is an instrument of emancipation. It is the affirmation that digital sovereignty is not an abstract concept, but a solid, tangible structure that you control from end to end.
